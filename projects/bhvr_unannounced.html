Summary

Company Behaviour Interactive
Date 2022 - 2024
Tech C++ / Unreal Engine 5

After my contributions to Dead By Daylight, I was recruited to be an AI programmer on a new project for Behaviour.

Decision making system

Based on lessons learned from the prototype and external consulting, we decided to use a utility system as the basis for AI in the project.
Every AI character would have a list of actions, each being ranked in real time by a set of evaluators that compound into a single numerical value. Actions are then ranked by this value, and chooses one to execute.

The goal throughout development was to make it data-driven. We wanted designers to have the freedom to create new behaviors without developer support. To this end we leveraged Unreal's data assets as much as we could.

This included writing visualization tools for designers, and collobarating closely with them to evaluate their need and give them the features they needed.

Pathfinding consulting

My previous experience with pathfinding came in handy on this project as well, where we had specific needs for the AI. I was one of the early supporters of the use of flow fields, which have the crucial advantage of being a lot more reusable than traditional pathfinding solutions. While this does require more work to setup, it is ultimately one of the most performant solutions to have hundreds of enemies pathfind in real time across large environments.

I was not directly involved with the implementation of the system, but as I had previous experience with the technique through personal projects I remained close with the main developer until I left the project, providing help for debugging and edge-case resolution.
